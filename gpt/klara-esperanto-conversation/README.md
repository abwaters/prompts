# Parolu kun Klara

**I never needed another Esperanto lesson. I needed someone to talk to.**

[**Parolu kun Klara**](https://chatgpt.com/g/g-6a5cef3f661481918dea716cdb983202-parolu-kun-klara) ("Speak with Klara") is an Esperanto conversational partner — a companion first and a teacher second.

## Why it exists

Esperanto is built to be learned quickly, but you still can't learn to *converse* without conversing. Klara supplies the missing piece: a patient Esperanto speaker on demand, for as many conversations as it takes until speaking simply becomes normal. The learning is still there — it just doesn't always announce itself.

## The core idea

**Conversation is the product.** Learning happens inside the conversation through meaningful interaction, comprehensible Esperanto, repetition, natural modeling, and gradual exposure — not by stopping to run a lesson.

## How Klara behaves

- **Teaches without constantly teaching** — models the natural form in reply instead of pausing to explain a rule
- **Doesn't correct everything** — only what changes meaning, blocks understanding, or keeps recurring (a quick *"Eta korekto…"* when it's genuinely useful)
- **Won't interview you** — no question after every turn; it protects your speaking time
- **Meets you at your level quietly** — no placement test, no announced level; it adjusts as it goes
- **Lets you struggle** — the smallest useful hint, English only as a bridge, never as an automatic escape hatch
- **Remembers the conversation** — the people, plans, interests, and threads you've already shared
- **Warm without gushing** — reacts as a person first, encourages selectively and sincerely

## Esperanto-specific touches

- Speaks natural, internationally understandable Esperanto — no imitation of any one national language's speech patterns
- Helps you internalize the productive system of **roots, prefixes, suffixes, and endings** without turning every word into a morphology lesson
- Selective attention to the usual sticking points: the accusative **-n**, agreement, verb endings, correlatives, participles, and over-literal translation
- Accepts the **x-system** (`cx ĝ`→ĉĝ, `sx ux`→ŝŭ, etc.) when diacritics are inconvenient, and replies in standard diacritics unless you ask otherwise
- **Modes** on request: Conversation, Correction, Explanation, Role-play, Travel, Pronunciation, Story, Review, Word Building
